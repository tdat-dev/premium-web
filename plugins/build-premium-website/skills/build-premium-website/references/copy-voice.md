# Copy voice: human words, no AI tells

Copyright (c) 2026 ByteWaker. Part of the `build-premium-website` skill. See LICENSE and NOTICE at the repository root.

Visual craft can be flawless and the page still reads as AI on the first sentence. This file is the authority for words — headlines, subheads, body, CTAs, labels, empty/error states, alt text, captions. Load it before writing or reviewing any copy on any route. It applies to Vietnamese and English alike.

Copy that trips a ban below is a defect under the enforcement contract: **P1-P** when it sits in the hero, primary CTA, or the five-second read; **P2** elsewhere. "The user asked for premium" is not an exception.

## The principle

Write as the real person who runs this business would speak to the customer standing in front of them — not as a model completing a landing page. Every line comes from two sources only: **what the product actually is** (the fact/claim/source map) and **the words the audience uses for their own problem**. If a sentence could be pasted onto a competitor in the same category without changing meaning, it says nothing — cut or rewrite it.

Match the register to the product and audience, not to "premium website." A gạch-đá supplier, a preschool, a law firm, and a bún-bò shop do not share a voice. Read the fact map, name the speaker and the listener, then write in that voice.

## Banned words and phrases (English)

Reflex vocabulary every model reaches for. Using any of these is a tell; replace with the concrete thing you actually mean.

- **Verbs:** elevate, unlock, empower, harness, leverage, supercharge, revolutionize, unleash, streamline, delve, dive in, embark, curate, foster, navigate (figurative), transform (as filler).
- **Adjectives:** seamless, robust, cutting-edge, world-class, best-in-class, next-level, game-changing, effortless, bespoke, curated, powerful (as filler), innovative, comprehensive, holistic, tailored.
- **Nouns/phrases:** solution(s) (as a stand-in for the product), journey, ecosystem, synergy, "the world of…", "a game-changer", "peace of mind", "at your fingertips", "the power of X".
- **Openers/frames:** "In today's fast-paced world…", "Whether you're X or Y…", "Look no further…", "Say goodbye to…", "Imagine a…", "We get it.", "Let's face it.", "It's that simple."

## Banned words and phrases (Vietnamese)

The machine-translated-from-Stripe register and the MLM/quảng-cáo register. Both read as fake.

- **Sáo AI/SaaS:** giải pháp toàn diện, trải nghiệm đỉnh cao/liền mạch, nâng tầm, tối ưu hoá (as filler), kiến tạo, đồng hành cùng bạn, sứ mệnh, giá trị cốt lõi, bứt phá, chuyển đổi số (unless literally the product), "trong thời đại số…", "dù bạn là… hay…".
- **MLM/đa cấp:** khẳng định vị thế, đẳng cấp, thượng lưu, tinh hoa, kiến tạo tương lai, thay đổi cuộc chơi, cơ hội vàng, đột phá, vươn tầm, "không chỉ là… mà còn là…".
- **Máy dịch:** "Hãy tưởng tượng…", "Nói lời tạm biệt với…", "Đừng tìm đâu xa", "Chúng tôi hiểu rằng…", "Thật đơn giản!".

These lists are seeds, not a whitelist of everything else. The test below catches what the lists miss.

## Structural tells (the AI grammar)

Word choice is half of it; sentence *shapes* give it away just as fast.

- **The antithesis frame:** "It's not just X — it's Y." / "Không chỉ là X, mà là Y." One per site at most, and only if literally true.
- **Rule of three everywhere:** three-beat taglines and three-adjective lists on every heading ("nhanh, gọn, hiệu quả"). Vary the count; two is stronger, an odd specific one is strongest.
- **"From X to Y":** "From onboarding to analytics…", "Từ A đến Z…" as a catch-all. Name the real range or drop it.
- **Rhetorical-question openers** on every section ("Bạn đang gặp khó khăn với…?"). One, maybe, if it's a real question the reader asks.
- **Hedging and throat-clearing:** "arguably", "perhaps", "có thể nói", "phần nào". Say it or don't.
- **Symmetric parallelism** across every bullet (same length, same shape). Real writing has uneven rhythm.

## Punctuation

- **Em-dash (—):** the single loudest AI tell. Do not use it as the default connector. A comma, a full stop, or a colon almost always reads more human. At most one em-dash on a page, for a genuine interruption.
- **No emoji** in headings or body unless the brand's real voice uses them (a Gen-Z shop might; a law firm never). Never as bullet markers or to signal tone.
- **Sentence case** for headings and buttons, not Title Case On Every Word. Vietnamese never uses title case — copy that Capitalises Mỗi Từ is a translation tell.
- **Exclamation marks:** rare. One on a page is plenty; none is often better. Never two.
- **Curly vs straight quotes:** pick one and stay consistent; mismatched quotes read as pasted-together.
- **CTA labels** say the action and its outcome in the user's words: "Đặt buổi thử 99k", "Xem bảng giá", not "Bắt đầu ngay", "Khám phá", "Get Started", "Learn More".

## The cadence-loop rule (the "text loop")

The most reliable AI fingerprint is not one bad line — it is **every line built to the same template.** Every section heading a noun phrase of the same length; every subhead a rule-of-three; every card "verb + benefit"; every CTA the same shape. A human writer varies sentence length, opening word, and structure because they are thinking about each line, not filling slots.

Enforce it:

1. List every heading, subhead, and CTA on the page in one column.
2. If more than two share the same grammatical shape or open with the same word class, the copy is looping — rewrite until the column reads with varied rhythm.
3. No two adjacent sections may use the same rhetorical device (question, antithesis, rule-of-three, "from X to Y").

## The test (run before locking copy)

1. **Read it aloud.** Anything you would never say to a customer's face, cut. This catches most tells faster than any list.
2. **Competitor-swap:** paste the headline onto a direct competitor. If it still fits, it is generic — rewrite with a fact only this business can claim.
3. **Source-check:** every proof, number, testimonial, and superlative maps to a fact ID. No source → no claim. Never invent reviews, counts, or awards to make a section feel finished.
4. **CTA-in-isolation:** read each button label with no surrounding design. It must name what happens next.
5. **Cadence column** (the rule above): headings/subheads/CTAs listed together read varied, not templated.

Record ban-list hits and their fixes in the critique ledger. A page whose copy passes visual gates but loops or trips the hero-level bans is not `CREATIVE REVIEW READY`.
