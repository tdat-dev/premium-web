# Reflex registry 2026

Last reviewed: 2026-07-14

Use this dated registry for greenfield work, creative art direction, and high-freedom redesigns. These are saturated 2025-2026 generator fingerprints, not eternal bans and not proof that a design is AI-made. A single signal may be the correct product choice.

The question is not "does this pattern exist?" It is "did the project earn this pattern through content, function, brand, or evidence?"

## Review protocol

1. Mark only signals visible in the proposed or rendered direction.
2. At **two signals on the same surface or narrative sequence**, trigger an explicit reflex review. Record why each signal exists, the project fact it expresses, and a credible replacement considered.
3. At **three signals on the same surface or narrative sequence**, redesign that surface unless an evidence-based exception demonstrates that the combination is native to the brand/product.
4. Do not add unrelated signals merely to look different. The replacement must improve specificity, clarity, or function.
5. Store the decision in the decision record and verify the rendered result.

The two/three-signal thresholds are intervention triggers, not originality scores. They do not certify work above or below the threshold.

## Evidence-based exception

A saturated signal may stay when the record contains:

- the brand/product/content fact that requires or strongly supports it;
- why the pattern improves comprehension, function, recognition, or intended expression;
- at least one credible replacement considered;
- rendered evidence at relevant states and viewports;
- accessibility, responsive, performance, and failure implications;
- an external reviewer or user decision when three signals combine.

"It looks premium", "it is trending", "the reference used it", or "Awwwards style" is not evidence.

## Product-register exception

Some products natively belong to a saturated visual register: a developer terminal may use monospace and acid green; a retro game may use pixels; a glass-material product may demonstrate refraction; a publishing brand may own an italic serif; a dashboard may require cards.

In those cases:

1. Cite the product behavior, existing identity, content genre, or historical source.
2. Preserve usability and input parity.
3. Avoid adding unrelated co-trending signals merely to intensify the look.
4. Test whether the design remains recognizable when decorative effects are removed.

The exception applies to the justified register, not to every fashionable pattern associated with it.

## Annual refresh

Review this registry at least once per calendar year and whenever generator defaults materially shift.

- Add a signal only after repeated observation across current generators/templates and live sites.
- Retire or soften signals that are no longer saturated.
- Keep dated snapshots; do not silently rewrite prior context.
- Record the review date, evidence sample, additions, removals, and reviewer.
- Never convert a dated advisory into a permanent aesthetic ban.
